Saying that a constraint is value-consistent should then mean that it
exists a value for each involved variable such that the constraint is
satisfied.

Examples :
X in {1, 2, 3}
Y in {3, 4, 5}
the constraint X = Y is value-consistent.

X in {1, 2, 3}
Y in {4, 5, 6}
